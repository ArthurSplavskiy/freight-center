Before your ship your motorcyle

  • Rev your engine

    Make sure your motorcycle starts, runs properly and the battery is in good condition

  • Repair any leaks

    Be sure your bike is safe to ship by repairing oil and fuel leaks or reporting them. Some carriers can put a catch under your bike to protect from minor leaks during transport

  • Measure your bike

    Make sure the overall length of your motorcycle is less than 99 inches, the length of a stock FLH with hard bags and trunk. Extra long pipes may require removal. If your bike cannot be made shorter than 99 inches, give us a call

  • Run it low

    Before your bike is loaded on the truck, be sure to run your motorcycle low on fuel. You’ll want less than one gallon of fuel in the tank

  • Fuel injection

    If you do not have fuel injection, at time of loading it is necessary to shut your fuel petcock off and run the carburetor low or out of fuel to protect from overflow during transport

  • Free your forks

    Do not lock your forks or steering column or they will have to be cut/opened at your expense
